13.1 Introduction

Sampling is the process of selecting a small part (sample) from a population to draw conclusions about the whole.

13.2 Population

Population is the complete set of all possible observations.

  • Finite Population
  • Infinite Population
  • Real Population
  • Hypothetical Population
Key Terms:
Population Size (N), Parameter (μ, σ)

13.3 Sample

A subset of population selected for study.

  • Sample Size (n)
  • Statistic (e.g., mean, SD)
  • Estimator & Estimate

13.4 Survey Techniques

1) Census Survey

  • Studies entire population
  • High accuracy
  • Time & cost intensive

2) Sampling Survey

  • Studies part of population
  • Less costly & faster
  • Widely used

13.5 Methods of Sampling

Non-Probability Sampling

  • Judgment Sampling
  • Quota Sampling
  • Convenience Sampling

Probability Sampling

  • Simple Random Sampling
  • Stratified Sampling
  • Systematic Sampling
  • Cluster Sampling

Simple Random Sampling

  • Equal chance for all units
  • Lottery method
  • Random number method

Stratified Sampling

Population divided into homogeneous groups (strata).

Systematic Sampling

Select every k-th unit after random start.

Cluster Sampling

Population divided into clusters and entire cluster selected.

13.6 Sampling Errors

Sampling Error: Difference between sample statistic and population parameter.
  • Decreases as sample size increases
  • Absent in census
Non-Sampling Error: Errors due to data collection, processing, etc.
  • Occurs in both census & sampling
  • May increase with sample size

Summary

  • Sampling saves time and cost
  • Various methods exist for selection
  • Errors must be minimized for accuracy
