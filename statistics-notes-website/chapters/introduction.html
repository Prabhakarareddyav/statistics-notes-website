1.1 Introduction

Statistics plays a vital role in modern science, business, agriculture, medicine, and technology. Originally statistics was related to state administration but today it is used in almost every field to analyze data and support decision making.

Statistics is mainly concerned with collecting, organizing, analyzing and interpreting numerical data.

Examples of statistical data include:
  • Population growth
  • Crop production
  • Business profits
  • Medical research results

1.2 Meaning of Statistics

The word statistics is used in two senses.

Statistics in Singular Sense

Statistics refers to a branch of science dealing with methods of collecting, presenting, analyzing and interpreting data.

Statistics in Plural Sense

Statistics refers to numerical facts and figures arranged systematically.

Examples:

  • Population statistics
  • Economic growth data
  • Agricultural production statistics

1.3 Definitions of Statistics

Statistician Definition
Croxton and Cowden Statistics is the science of collecting, presenting, analyzing and interpreting numerical data.
R.A. Fisher Statistics is a branch of applied mathematics dealing with observational data.
Lovitt Statistics deals with classification and tabulation of numerical facts.
A.L. Bowley Statistics is the science of counting and measuring social phenomena.

1.4 Types of Statistics

Descriptive Statistics

Descriptive statistics summarizes and presents data using tables, charts and averages.

Examples include:
  • Mean
  • Median
  • Mode
  • Charts and graphs

Inferential Statistics

Inferential statistics uses sample data to draw conclusions about a population.

Examples include:
  • Hypothesis testing
  • Regression analysis
  • Confidence intervals

1.5 Nature of Statistics

Statistics is both a science and an art.

Statistics as Science

  • Uses systematic methods
  • Follows scientific principles
  • Analyzes relationships among variables

Statistics as Art

  • Requires skill in interpretation
  • Requires experience in data analysis

1.6 Aims of Statistics

  • To study populations
  • To understand variation in data
  • To summarize large amounts of information
  • To assist in decision making

1.7 Functions of Statistics

  • Simplifies complex data
  • Facilitates comparison
  • Helps in forecasting future trends
  • Tests scientific hypotheses
  • Supports policy decisions

1.8 Applications of Statistics

Government

  • Budget preparation
  • Economic planning
  • Population studies

Business

  • Market research
  • Demand forecasting
  • Production planning

Medicine

  • Drug testing
  • Disease analysis

Agriculture

  • Crop yield experiments
  • Soil classification
  • Fertilizer research

1.9 Limitations of Statistics

  • Statistics deals only with numerical data.
  • It studies aggregates rather than individuals.
  • Results are approximate and not exact.
  • Statistics can be misused if interpreted incorrectly.

Chapter Summary

Statistics is a powerful tool used for collecting, analyzing and interpreting data. It has wide applications in government, business, agriculture and science. Despite some limitations, statistical methods are essential for research and decision making.
